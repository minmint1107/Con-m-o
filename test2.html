Temperate forest

Location distribution on Earth:

• North America, Europe, Asia, and Australasia. Some of the most well-known temperate forests include the deciduous forests of eastern North America, the beech forests of Europe, and the mixed forests of Japan.

Characteristic plant species:

• Temperate forests are home to a wide variety of plant species, including deciduous trees such as oak, maple, and beech, as well as conifers such as spruce, fir, and pine. Understory plants such as ferns, mosses, and wildflowers are also common in temperate forests.

Typical animals:

• Temperate forests are home to many different types of animals, including mammals such as bears, deer, wolves, and raccoons, as well as birds such as owls, hawks, and woodpeckers.

• Reptiles such as snakes and lizards, and amphibians such as frogs and salamanders can also be found in temperate forests.

• In addition, many species of insects and other invertebrates live in the forest, including butterflies, beetles, and spiders.

Flying squirrel

• Food: Flying squirrels eat insects, fruits, and seeds.

• Distribution: Flying squirrels live mainly in Southeast Asia, including India, China and Japan, in tropical forests and open forests.

• Appearance: Flying squirrels are small, 25 to 40 cm long, with a long tail and skin membranes that help them fly. Colouration varies depending on the species but is usually grey or brown with white or black spots on the feathers. They have large eyes and pointed ears.

Tropical rain forest

Location distribution on Earth:

• Tropical rainforests are found in regions near the equator, where temperatures and rainfall are high.

• They are primarily located in Central and South America, West and Central Africa, Southeast Asia, and parts of Australia.

Characteristic plant species:

• Tropical rainforests are home to an incredible variety of plant species, including tall trees like mahogany, kapok, and rubber trees, as well as smaller plants like ferns, vines, and orchids.

• The forest floor is often covered with a thick layer of leaves and other organic matter, which provides nutrients for the plants.

Typical animals:

• Tropical rainforests are home to a vast array of animal species, including mammals like monkeys, sloths, jaguars, and tapirs.

• Birds like parrots, toucans, and hummingbirds are also common in these forests.

• Reptiles like snakes, lizards, and turtles, as well as amphibians like frogs and salamanders, are also found in tropical rainforests.

• Food: Insects

• Distribution: Central and South America

• Appearance:Small, brightly colored frogs with a distinctive pattern of spots.

Desert

Location on Earth:

• Found on every continent except Europe. The largest deserts are located in the subtropics and cover about onethird of the Earth's surface.

Characteristics of plants:

• Cacti, succulents, and other plants that are adapted to survive in arid conditions.

Typical animals:

• Snakes, lizards, coyotes, camels, and other animals that are adapted to survive in hot and dry conditions.
